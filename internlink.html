About the Project

Internlink was a personality based job matching platform. We used a psychometric analysis to link students with their most compatible intership and job opportunities.

Visit Project

*Design doc only (company dissolved)

The problem

Taking a product from an idea to over 500 users.

One of three columns

Prototyping, Building, & Iterating

Creating this product required lots of user research and testing. We had three primary users, students, hiring managers, and colleges. Each presented their own challenges and motivations. After doing some initial user reseach, I quickly created some (hidius) mockups that allowed us to get it in the hands of our users. We uncovered many issues with our workflows and the biggest being the time it took to take the assement. I worked with our team of psychologists to reword and shorten the assement without compromising its integrity. From there I made some UX/UI changes to make things more modern and made the pocess of uploading a job much easier for hiring managers. This is also the time that I learned full-stack web development at General Assembly. After I tested a new feature with our users, I would then code it, contributing to the frontend of a LAMP stack.

The Final Design

The final design can be seen in [FIGURE 21]. The team and I solved many of the issues for all of our user types. I managed the development of the product, the internal internship program, as well as all testing and frontend design of the product. A few months after our product was complete we grew to 500 users. After growing we secured angel funding from investors who wanted to contribute not just financially but with their expertise as well. Shortly after securing the funding I discovered a string of less than honorable acts by another founder. This is when I decided it was best to leave the project. It was a great product with lots of potential and I learned lessons that helped me in all future projects.
